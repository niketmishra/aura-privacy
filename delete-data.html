How to delete
your data.

Aura keeps your data on your phone, not on a server. Deleting it is fast, immediate, and entirely under your control. Here is exactly how.

Two ways to delete

  1. From inside the app (recommended) Open Aura → tap the You tab in the bottom bar → tap Privacy → tap Delete account → confirm. Every byte of your data is wiped immediately, and the app returns to its first-launch state. This is the fastest path and we encourage you to use it.
  2. By uninstalling Aura Uninstalling the app from your phone removes all on-device storage, which is where Aura keeps everything. There is no Aura server holding a copy. Once uninstalled, nothing of yours remains anywhere.

What gets deleted

Both deletion paths above wipe the following data immediately:

  • Your readings (all playlists Aura has generated for you).
  • Your mood history (every mood you have selected, and when).
  • Your taste profile (inferred top artists, genres, languages, and adventurousness).
  • Marginalia (private notes you have written on individual tracks).
  • Liked tracks and disliked tracks.
  • Your display name.
  • OAuth refresh tokens for Google (YouTube) and Spotify, if you connected those accounts.
  • Any cached catalogue data (artist images, track metadata).

If you used the in-app "Delete account" option (Step 1 above), Aura also issues a best-effort revocation of your Google OAuth grant at Google's servers, so the app's access to your YouTube account is terminated at Google's end as well.

What is kept, and for how long

0 days
On-device user data (readings, mood history, taste profile, marginalia, display name, OAuth tokens): deleted the moment you tap "Delete account" or uninstall the app. Aura keeps no backup.
90 days
Anonymized crash reports sent to Sentry (sentry.io), our crash-tracking service. Crash reports contain the error message, app version, device model, and a randomly-generated session ID. They do not contain your name, email, OAuth tokens, marginalia, or taste profile. Sentry deletes them automatically after 90 days. You can request earlier deletion by emailing the address below.
Until you revoke
Your existing Google and Spotify accounts (if you connected them) remain on those services' servers, owned by you. These are not Aura's data. Manage them at myaccount.google.com and spotify.com/account/apps. Aura's in-app delete revokes the Google grant on a best-effort basis; Spotify does not provide a programmatic revoke endpoint, so you would revoke that one yourself in Spotify's dashboard.

If you cannot access the app

If you have already uninstalled Aura or lost access to your device, there is nothing left for us to delete from your side — your data lived on your phone and was removed when the app was uninstalled.

If you would still like a written confirmation of deletion, or if you want anonymized crash reports purged from Sentry sooner than the 90-day automatic deletion, email us:
